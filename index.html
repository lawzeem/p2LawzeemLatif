Part 1

Below are some of the Dashboards I found online. Each has their own Pros and Cons.

Analog Dashboard

Pros

  • All Information is Present, not hidden under complicated menu.
  • Units of measurement are fixed. Could be an issue with different regions.

    • Cons

  • Too many dials, so confusing for new users.
  • Small fonts in some areas, not user-friendly or accessible.
  • Over time the glass/screen gets dirty and the lights gets diffused. Not futureproof.

    • Sporty Dashboard

    Pros

  • This is a dashboard for professionals who need all the information at once.
  • The most important gauge for the driver, the tachometer, is large and in focus.
  • Less important dials are off to the side taking up less space.

    • Cons

  • Some of the dials are hidden because of the steering wheel.
  • Some of the font used is extremely small.
  • The color of the text in the speedometer is hard to read.

    • Backlit Dashboard

    Pros

  • Important gauge is bigger so its easier to read.
  • Lighting makes it easier to read at night.
  • Useful information like gear and outside temperature is also present.

    • Cons

  • Units of measurement are not user adjustable.
  • Fuel gauge measurement is not precise.

    • Partially Digital Dashboard

    Pros

  • Center display provides useful information.
  • Easy to read at night.

    • Cons

  • Units are not user adjustable.
  • Fuel gauge is small and may be an issue for users.
  • Both dials are the same size but speedometer is more dense with information, could make that bigger to space it out.

    • Digital Dashboard

    Pros

  • User customizable, user can select font, size and color to suit their needs.

    • Cons

  • Some items could be hidden under menus which can be confusing and distracting while driving.

    • Part 2

    Common Features of Car Dashboard

  • Check the speed of the car.
  • Check the status of the car with various check engine, emergency lights, tire pressure.
  • Check if everything is ready before driving, like doors closed, seatbelts.

    • Rare Uses of Car Dashboard

  • Check outside temperature.
  • Miles traveled with the odometer. Only used while servicing or when thinking of servicing.
  • Check the time.

    • Controls used to interact with the dashboard

  • Little knobs on the dashboard.
  • Buttons on the steering wheel.

    • Common mistakes with current design

  • Confusing or missing labels on the controls.
  • Usually no way to reset changes.

    • Sketch of suggested Control Interface

    Justification for design

  • The turn signal and the wipers are in the usual place.
  • Having 2 blocks of buttons on the steering wheel accessible by the drivers thumbs make the buttons easy to find.
  • The driver will not have to fiddle too much and can easily press any button needed.

    • Part 3

    Proposed Dashboard Design

  • This design keeps things organized.
  • The speedometer is the center of attention. Everything else is off to its side.

    • Updated propsal for design

  • After some prototyping, the original design had too many lights in the top left "Engine Light" box.
  • This made the dashoard feel too cluttered so I moved some lights to next to the Left and Right Indicator.
  • This makes use of previously unused space while also giving some breathing room so the driver is not overwhelmed by all the signals.
